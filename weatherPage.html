    • What is correlation used for?

    Correlation is used to find the relationship between two quantitative variables that are linear.

    What is the difference between correlation and causation?

    Correlation does not imply causation. A change in the variable's linear line does not affect the other variable's linear line that happens to line up.

    We compare and contrast with different visually displayed relationships, the parallel coordinate lines are used for plotting multivariate, numerical data and a scatter plot matrix to see the relationship between combination of variables, we can avoid the causation vs correlation.
